# Key Authors in Open Source Law

> Last updated: 2026-03-15

This directory identifies the leading scholars and practitioners whose work forms the foundation of the Virtual OSS Attorney's knowledge base.

## Practitioners

### Heather Meeker
- **Affiliation:** Tech Law Partners LLP (formerly O'Melveny & Myers, DLA Piper)
- **Key Work:** *Open Source for Business: A Practical Guide to Open Source Software Licensing* (3rd ed., 2020) — the leading practical guide for companies using OSS
- **Expertise:** License compliance, dual licensing, business models, commercial OSS strategy
- **Other:** Drafted the Elastic License 2.0, PolyForm advisor
- **Publications:** UC Hastings Science & Technology Law Journal; numerous articles and conference presentations

### Lawrence Rosen
- **Affiliation:** Rosenlaw & Einschlag (retired)
- **Key Work:** *Open Source Licensing: Software Freedom and Intellectual Property Law* (Prentice Hall, 2004)
- **Expertise:** License drafting (authored Academic Free License, Open Software License), derivative works analysis, IP theory
- **Other:** Former general counsel of the Open Source Initiative

### Eben Moglen
- **Affiliation:** Columbia Law School; Software Freedom Law Center (SFLC) founder
- **Key Work:** GPL drafting (GPLv3 lead drafter), FSF legal counsel
- **Expertise:** Free software philosophy, copyleft theory, GPL enforcement strategy
- **Publications:** "Enforcing the GNU GPL" (FSF); numerous speeches and articles

### Van Lindberg
- **Affiliation:** Attorney and engineer; former VP/AGC at Rackspace
- **Key Work:** *Intellectual Property and Open Source: A Practical Guide to Protecting Code* (O'Reilly, 2008)
- **Expertise:** Patent law for developers, IP strategy, OSS business models
- **Other:** Co-chairs PLI's Open Source Software program with Heather Meeker

### Andrew Katz
- **Affiliation:** Moorcrofts LLP (UK)
- **Key Work:** EU open source legal perspective, EUPL analysis, open hardware licensing
- **Expertise:** European OSS law, FSFE Legal Network, CRA compliance
- **Other:** Regular contributor to IFOSS Law Review / JOLTS

### Richard Fontana
- **Affiliation:** Red Hat / IBM (in-house counsel)
- **Key Work:** License drafting — GPLv3 (contributor), MPL 2.0 (contributor)
- **Expertise:** Copyleft licensing, weak copyleft, OSS governance, open standards
- **Other:** Active in community license development and review

### Karen Copenhaver
- **Affiliation:** Choate Hall & Stewart LLP (partner)
- **Key Work:** Linux Foundation legal advisor, OSS governance counsel
- **Expertise:** Foundation governance, IP policy design, corporate OSS strategy
- **Other:** Founding board member of the Open Invention Network

### Pamela Chestek
- **Affiliation:** Chestek Legal (sole practitioner)
- **Key Work:** Trademark law and open source
- **Expertise:** OSS trademark policy, Model Trademark Guidelines, OSI board
- **Other:** Leading voice on trademark issues specific to open source projects

### Bradley Kuhn
- **Affiliation:** Software Freedom Conservancy (Policy Fellow, former President)
- **Key Work:** Copyleft enforcement philosophy, GPL compliance
- **Expertise:** Copyleft enforcement, community-oriented enforcement, fiscal sponsorship
- **Other:** Advocate for principled GPL enforcement; critic of CLA-enabled relicensing

### Kyle Mitchell
- **Affiliation:** Independent attorney and license drafter
- **Key Work:** Blue Oak Model License, PolyForm licenses, Prosperity Public License, Parity Public License
- **Expertise:** Modern license drafting, source-available licensing, developer-accessible legal writing
- **Publications:** writing.kemitchell.com — extensive blog on OSS legal topics

### Till Jaeger
- **Affiliation:** JBB Rechtsanwälte (Berlin, Germany)
- **Key Work:** German GPL enforcement (counsel for Harald Welte/gpl-violations.org)
- **Expertise:** European OSS enforcement, German copyright law applied to OSS
- **Other:** Co-editor of IFOSS Law Review / JOLTS

### Luis Villa
- **Affiliation:** Tidelift (co-founder)
- **Key Work:** OSS licensing evolution, sustainability models
- **Expertise:** Modern license design, OSAID debate, open source sustainability
- **Other:** Former counsel at Wikimedia Foundation and Mozilla

## Scholars

### Pamela Samuelson
- **Affiliation:** UC Berkeley School of Law (Richard M. Sherman Distinguished Professor)
- **Key Work:** Software copyright, digital copyright, fair use in technology
- **Expertise:** Foundational scholarship on software IP, amicus work in Oracle v. Google
- **Publications:** Berkeley Technology Law Journal; Texas Law Review; numerous articles on software copyright

### Mark Lemley
- **Affiliation:** Stanford Law School (William H. Neukom Professor of Law)
- **Key Work:** "Software Patents and the Return of Functional Claiming" (Wisconsin Law Review, 2013)
- **Expertise:** Patent law, software patents, IP and competition, antitrust
- **Other:** Among the most-cited IP scholars; significant influence on court decisions

### Jorge L. Contreras
- **Affiliation:** University of Utah S.J. Quinney College of Law
- **Key Work:** "A Market Reliance Theory for FRAND Commitments and Other Patent Pledges" (Utah Law Review, 2015)
- **Expertise:** Patent pledges, FRAND commitments, SEPs, standards and IP
- **Other:** Created the Patent Pledge Database; leading scholar on patent commons

## Institutions

### Software Freedom Conservancy (SFC)
- Fiscal sponsor for ~40 OSS projects (Git, Homebrew, Selenium, etc.)
- Copyleft enforcement program
- Publishes principles of community-oriented GPL enforcement
- sfconservancy.org

### Free Software Foundation (FSF)
- Steward of the GPL, LGPL, AGPL, and GFDL
- GPL enforcement (primarily through private negotiation)
- Maintains the Free Software Definition and GPL FAQ
- fsf.org

### Open Source Initiative (OSI)
- Steward of the Open Source Definition
- License review and approval process
- Maintains the approved license list
- opensource.org

### FSFE (Free Software Foundation Europe)
- European Legal Network (400+ FOSS lawyers)
- REUSE initiative for license compliance
- Annual Legal & Licensing Workshop
- fsfe.org

### Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF)
- Digital rights advocacy including OSS-related issues
- Amicus briefs in key software IP cases (Oracle v. Google, etc.)
- eff.org

### JOLTS / IFOSS Law Review
- Premier peer-reviewed journal for FOSS legal analysis
- Open access (CC-BY 4.0)
- Successor to IFOSS Law Review (rebranded 2019)
- jolts.world
