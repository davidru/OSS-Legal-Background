# EU & International Open Source Case Law Index

> Last updated: 2026-03-15

## EU Cases

### CJEU (Court of Justice of the European Union)
| Case | Citation | Key Issue | Holding |
|------|----------|-----------|---------|
| **SAS v. WPL** | **C-406/10 (2012)** | **Software copyright scope** | **Functionality, programming language, and data formats are not copyrightable** |
| Huawei v. ZTE | C-170/13 (2015) | SEP injunctions / FRAND | Framework for SEP holder injunctive relief |

### Germany
| Case | Year | Court | Key Issue | Outcome |
|------|------|-------|-----------|---------|
| Welte v. Sitecom | 2004 | LG München I | First GPL enforcement | Injunction granted — GPL enforceable |
| Welte v. Fortinet | 2005 | LG München I | GPL + encryption | Injunction — source must be provided |
| Welte v. D-Link | 2006 | LG Frankfurt | GPL enforcement | GPL enforced — landmark "acid test" |
| Welte v. Skype | 2007 | LG München I | GPL in consumer devices | Injunction granted |
| Welte v. Fantec | 2013 | LG Hamburg | Distributor obligations | Distributors must fulfill GPL (not just manufacturers) |
| **Hellwig v. VMware** | **2016/2019** | **LG/OLG Hamburg** | **Kernel modules + derivative works** | **Dismissed on evidentiary grounds — substance unresolved** |
| McHardy v. Geniatech | 2018 | LG Mannheim | GPL troll enforcement | Controversial serial enforcement |

### Key German Legal Framework
- **UrhG §§ 69a-69g:** Software copyright provisions implementing EU Software Directive
- GPL treated as enforceable license under German copyright law
- Breach of license conditions = copyright infringement (not just contract breach)
- Preliminary injunctions readily available

## China

| Case | Year | Court | Key Issue | Holding |
|------|------|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Wangjing v. Yibang** | **2021** | **Supreme People's Court** | **GPL + copyright ownership** | Copyright ownership separate from GPL compliance; GPL enforceable |
| Virtual App case | 2023 | — | GPL3.0 as contract | GPL has contractual force under Chinese law |
| Changsha Mitop cases | 2022 | — | OSS fishing litigation | OSS licenses valid; reduced abusive damages |

### Key Chinese Principles
- GPL treated as contract under Chinese Copyright Law and Civil Code
- Breach triggers both copyright and contractual remedies
- Supreme People's Court has established clear precedent for GPL enforceability
- OpenAtom Foundation maintains OSS legal database

| Regulation | Year | Key Impact on OSS |

| **Software Directive** (2009/24/EC) | 2009 (codified) | Copyright framework for all software including OSS |

| Framework | Year | Purpose |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Kernel Enforcement Statement | 2017 | GPLv3-style cure provisions for Linux kernel |

## Other Jurisdictions

Limited published case law in Japan, India, Brazil, and South Korea on open source specifically. These jurisdictions generally enforce OSS licenses under existing copyright and contract law frameworks but have not produced landmark decisions comparable to the US, Germany, or China.

## Key Themes

1. **GPL is enforceable globally.** US, Germany, and China have all confirmed GPL enforceability.
2. **Enforcement standing matters.** Individual contributors must prove their specific code is in the accused product (Hellwig v. VMware lesson).
3. **The derivative works question is globally unresolved.** No court anywhere has decided the linking question.
4. **Community enforcement norms matter.** The Kernel Enforcement Statement and GPL Cooperation Commitment have become de facto standards.
5. **Regulatory pressure is increasing.** The EU CRA and Product Liability Directive create new obligations around OSS security and liability.
