# Copyright Assignment in Open Source

> Last updated: 2026-03-15

## Summary

Copyright assignment is the transfer of copyright ownership from a contributor to a project entity (typically a foundation or company). Unlike copyright license CLAs (which grant a license but retain contributor ownership), copyright assignment transfers full ownership — the entity becomes the copyright holder.

Copyright assignment is the strongest form of IP consolidation in open source and the most controversial. It enables unified enforcement, relicensing, and IP management, but concentrates power in a single entity.

## Key Principles

- Copyright assignment transfers ownership; copyright license grants permission
- Only the copyright holder (or assignee) can enforce copyright — assignment consolidates enforcement power
- Assignment must be in writing (17 U.S.C. § 204(a))
- Assignment can be revoked by the author after 35 years (17 U.S.C. § 203) — a largely untested provision for software
- Foreign contributors may be subject to different assignment rules under their national law

## Models

### FSF Copyright Assignment
- Requires contributors to assign copyright in their contributions to the FSF
- FSF promises to always distribute under a free software license
- Enables unified GPL enforcement by a single entity
- Controversial: concentrates power in a single organization

### Canonical Contributor Agreement
- Canonical's CLA for Ubuntu and related projects includes copyright assignment provisions
- Has been criticized as more corporate-favorable than the FSF model
- Enables Canonical to dual-license or relicense

### Eclipse Foundation Contributor Agreement
- License-based CLA (not assignment) — contributors retain copyright
- The Eclipse Foundation receives a broad license to use and sublicense

## Practical Guidance

1. **Assignment is rarely necessary.** For most projects, a copyright license CLA (Apache model) provides sufficient rights without the controversy of assignment.
2. **Assignment enables unified enforcement.** If GPL enforcement is important, having a single copyright holder (via assignment) simplifies litigation.
3. **Assignment creates relicensing power.** The entity can change the license. This can be positive (upgrading GPL versions) or negative (relicensing to proprietary).
